About The Heroes

Andragon the Peaceful

Andragon was the one who brought all the Heroes together to protect the realm from the Doom. He fought only when it was the sole option and none dared to challenge him alone. His greatsword, the Edge could cut down any tree and his kite shield, the Wall withstood everything that came its way.

 

Shiila the Snake

Shiila was always ready to hunt her enemies and never backed away from a duel. She fought with her polearm, the Fang and with it she had unrivaled reach. Her smile was unforgettable as were her green, glimmering eyes.

 

Ingvir the Elder

Ingvir was older than anyone ever was, yet she looked like a thirty-year-old beauty. She was a mage and the first one to discover the Doom. Her knowledge of spells and magic made her invalueble to the Heroes.

 

Rummlad the Redwood

Rummlad was the best friend and the worst enemy one could have. The long beard was his trademark and some say it was the source of his powers. He had two devastating axes, the Right and the Left, although no one else was able to tell them apart.

 

Ghost the Unseen

Ghost was maybe the most famous of all the heroes but nobody knew much about the Unseen. Though everyone could tell you something about the nasty daggers even if they never had seen one.

 

Gilligon the Wind

Gilligon was a lonewolf. He did not really mind other people but being by himself came more naturally. It was told that he never missed with his red bow, the Devil. His shots were too fast to be seen but the sound they made was like a howling wind

 

The Doom

The Doom has many faces, all of them bring terror.

How to inform oncoming danger?

Here are the three most used ways. You have to take into account where you are and the distances between different groups.

  • Bells

    Big belltowers have been placed near strategically important places in the World.

  • Fire

    Fires can be seen faraway and a few places have been prepared with big firewood.

  • Crows

    Crows are loyal messengers and they can bring the information the farthest of all the three methods.
